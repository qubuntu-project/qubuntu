# QUBUNTU Impact Statement

## The Quantum Threat Reality

Current encryption methods protecting our digital infrastructure will become obsolete when quantum computers reach sufficient computational power. This isn't a distant future scenario - major tech companies and governments are actively developing quantum systems that could break RSA, ECC, and other widely-used cryptographic standards within the next 10-15 years.

## Critical Gap in the Market

While post-quantum cryptography standards (ML-KEM-768, ML-DSA-65) have been finalized by NIST, no production-ready Linux distribution currently implements these standards by default. Organizations and individuals remain vulnerable to future quantum attacks because:

- Existing distributions rely on legacy encryption
- Migration to quantum-safe systems requires extensive technical expertise
- No accessible, tested platform exists for quantum-safe computing
